Retail chains pivoting online


People visit eCommerce stores to find the best price, availability and delivery options.
There is still a large gap between these two activities, with the exception of high loyalty from retail stores transitioning to online stores.
Visual Catalogs (Virtual Reality based product display and picking) looks to fall somewhere in between current online offerings and vastly more expensive and disappearing retail store options.
While the overall market is large, our target market is retail chains that plan to mix fewer physical stores with more dynamic online options.

Evolving user interfaces

Our approach at this time is focused first on mobile, including using device orientation (Mild Orientation UI?) and animation (Follow UI?).

Automation and AI creating new roles


As AI replaces phone support and retail stores close low wage employees will seek new roles, our pipeline utilizes these people minor periodic training to produce results.

Incorporate human touch and remove complexity


We’re building a system that
Utilizes existing catalog creation processes, data and systems for eCommerce
Utilizes retail store SKU vs physical bin location arrangements
Team composition and full cycle delivery time from training through results. The cost and time to scale the business by utilizing short training cycles for all roles. Depending on non engineering personnel to do the vast majority of customizations (arrangements and asset creation and maintenance),the availability of people is much cheaper.

Empower local teams with renewing techniques

Local is important when it comes to training and teaming. Working remote from the office is fine, but not remote from the team or area. With employee turnover and skills that require regular renewing, it’s especially important to have employee to employee assistance. The social aspect in collaborating and teaching.

Effective technology and commerce experience

Write once, run everywhere.
Our focus is process engineering - forming, tooling, training and fueling our teams to produce results. At every step of our process tasks, coding and technique don’t require top level talent or skill. Non engineering tasks are short duration that depend on skills that can be learned and renewed with minor training. Virtual Reality tools, formats and techniques are in motion and will require teams to make ongoing improvements in technique and results.
