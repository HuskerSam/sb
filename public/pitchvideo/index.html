Stores are closing

The gaps between retail stores, printed catalogs and eCommerce
People view arrangements in store
Retail closings have been ongoing and with Covid have accelerated.

Printing and mailing

Mailed circular advertisements and printed catalogs still filling mail boxes and trash bins.
Eliminate mailed catalogs - existing ecommerce sites look “nothing like” actual stores or mailed catalogs
Paperless is more common now then not.

Experience gap

Existing eCommerce sites are 2D and lack the spatial arrangement and human touch that goes into retail displays, leaving a large gap between an in store experience and a website visit.
Retail stores price and convenience are losing ground versus eCommerce websites and applications, but the human touch with selecting and displaying products is still important with many categories of products.

Virtual Reality cloud

By offering a new option in between physical retail stores and eCommerce sites combining the spatial arrangement of products in a catalog and/or physical store location using Virtual Reality (on mobile first, but also other devices including console and headsets). It’s time to get more efficient on product presentation with eCommerce to offset the physical retail store collapse. At the same time representing products in arrangements and groups is a very common practice both in creating catalogs and retail store layouts.

Retail chains pivoting online


People visit retail stores to view products and layouts (displays). Most regular customers buy from stores on each visit out of appreciation for the retail selection and arrangement services.
People visit eCommerce stores to find the best price, availability and delivery options. There is still a large gap between these two activities, with the exception of high loyalty from retail stores transitioning to online stores.
Visual Catalogs (Virtual Reality based product display and picking) looks to fall somewhere in between current online offerings and vastly more expensive and disappearing retail store options.
While the overall market is large, our target market is retail chains that plan to mix fewer physical stores with more dynamic online options.

Evolving user interfaces

Our approach at this time is focused first on mobile, including using device orientation (Mild Orientation UI?) and animation (Follow UI?).

Automation and AI creating new roles


As AI replaces phone support and retail stores close low wage employees will seek new roles, our pipeline utilizes these people minor periodic training to produce results.

Enable existing catalog teams


We’re building a system that
Utilizes existing catalog creation processes, data and systems for eCommerce
Utilizes retail store SKU vs physical bin location arrangements
Team composition and full cycle delivery time from training through results. The cost and time to scale the business by utilizing short training cycles for all roles. Depending on non engineering personnel to do the vast majority of customizations (arrangements and asset creation and maintenance),the availability of people is much cheaper.

Local teams renewing techniques

Local is important when it comes to training and teaming. Working remote from the office is fine, but not remote from the team or area. With employee turnover and skills that require regular renewing, it’s especially important to have employee to employee assistance. The social aspect in collaborating and teaching.
